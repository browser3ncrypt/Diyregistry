Important: Adoption records are sealed in most states. Access is restricted to the adoptee (usually age 18 or 21+), birth parents (under certain conditions), or by court order. Many states offer mutual consent registries, non-identifying information, or limited OBC (original birth certificate) access for adult adoptees. County courts, adoption agencies, and private intermediaries may also hold records. Always verify current law and eligibility directly with the listed agency.

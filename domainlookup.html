What is a TLD?

A Top-Level Domain (TLD) is the last part of a domain name — the letters that come after the final dot. Examples include .com, .org, .net, and newer options like .app, .dev, .shop, and .xyz.

TLDs help define the purpose, location, or brand of a website. With hundreds of options now available, you can find a domain that perfectly matches your project, business, or personal brand.
